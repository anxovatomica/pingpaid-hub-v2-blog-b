How to Calculate Late Fees (Legally)

Published June 2026 · Freelance Finance · 6 min read

Your client is late. Again. You want to charge a late fee but you're not sure if it's legal, how much to charge, or how to calculate it.

Here's everything you need to know.

📜 Is It Legal to Charge Late Fees?

Yes. In most jurisdictions, you can charge late fees as long as:

  • The fee is stated in your contract or invoice before the work begins
  • The fee is "reasonable" (not punitive)
  • You comply with any state-specific limits
⚠️ Important: Some states have maximum allowable interest rates. Charging more could make your fee unenforceable or even illegal. Check your state's usury laws.

🧮 The Formula: Simple Interest

The standard formula for late fees is simple interest:

Late Fee = Principal × Rate × (Days Late ÷ 365)

Example Calculation

Let's say you invoiced $5,000 and the client is 30 days late. Your late fee rate is 1.5% per month (18% APR):

Late Fee = $5,000 × 0.18 × (30 ÷ 365) = $73.97

So the total due is $5,073.97.

💡 Pro tip: 1.5% per month (18% APR) is the sweet spot. It's high enough to motivate payment but not so high that clients feel gouged or that courts would consider it punitive.

📝 What to Include in Your Invoice

Your late fee policy should be clearly stated on every invoice. Here's the language I use:

A late fee of 1.5% per month (18% APR) will be applied to invoices paid more than 15 days after the due date.

📧 Email Templates for Late Payments

Day 1 (Due Date Passed)

Hi [Client Name], Just a friendly reminder that invoice [INV-001] for $[Amount] was due on [Date]. Please let me know if you need anything from me to process this payment. Thanks! [Your Name]

Day 15 (Late Fee About to Apply)

Hi [Client Name], Invoice [INV-001] for $[Amount] is now 15 days overdue. Per our agreement, a late fee of 1.5% per month will begin accruing tomorrow. Current total: $[Amount] After late fee (if paid today): $[Amount + Fee] Please let me know if there's an issue with the invoice or if you need a payment plan. Thanks, [Your Name]

Day 30 (Firm)

Hi [Client Name], Invoice [INV-001] is now 30 days overdue. Late fees have been applied per our agreement. Current total due: $[Amount + Fees] Late fees accrued: $[Fee Amount] Please remit payment by [Date] to avoid further escalation. I'm happy to discuss a payment plan if needed. [Your Name]
