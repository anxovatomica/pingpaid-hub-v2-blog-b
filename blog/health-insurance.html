💰 Tax Deductions

Health insurance premiums are 100% tax deductible for self-employed individuals. This includes:

  • Medical insurance premiums
  • Dental insurance premiums
  • Vision insurance premiums
  • Long-term care insurance (limits apply)
💡 Pro tip: The deduction is taken on Line 29 of Schedule 1 (Form 1040). You don't need to itemize. It reduces your adjusted gross income (AGI), which can qualify you for other tax benefits.

📊 How to Choose a Plan

  • Low deductible + high premium: Best if you have ongoing medical needs
  • High deductible + low premium: Best if you're healthy and have emergency savings
  • HSA eligible: Best if you want to save for retirement tax-free
  • PPO: Best if you want flexibility to see any doctor
  • HMO: Best if you want lower costs and don't mind referrals

🎯 Special Freelancer Considerations

  • Income fluctuation: ACA subsidies are based on estimated annual income. Underestimate carefully — you'll owe back subsidies if you earn more than projected.
  • No employer contribution: You pay 100% of the premium. Budget accordingly.
  • Pre-existing conditions: ACA plans must cover them. Short-term plans often don't.
  • Mental health: ACA plans must cover mental health services equally.
